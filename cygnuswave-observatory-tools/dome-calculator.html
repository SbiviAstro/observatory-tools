Formulas used
Up/Down = (mount axis height) − (dome center height)  ·  N/S and E/W = signed floor-mark distances  ·  GEM Axis Length = raw measurement + 0.5 × guidescope offset (if used)  ·  Radius = as measured (or diameter ÷ 2).

These are direct geometric inputs — NINA computes the live dome azimuth internally from these plus your mount's current RA/Dec and pier side. Once entered, turn on dome synchronization and nudge one value at a time only if you re-measure it and find it wrong; don't tune values arbitrarily to patch a specific slew position, or it'll get worse elsewhere in the sky.

3D Preview

3D Preview

● Clears ● Blocked ● Tight margin

Drag to orbit, scroll to zoom.

  • Wireframe dome — upper hemisphere, sitting on the wall down to your measured floor height
  • N / E / S / W — cardinal directions at the dome wall
  • Small bar — OTA
  • Amber line — optical axis
  • Glowing strip — the shutter door, full height, tracking azimuth only, like a real slit dome
  • Thin ring at the dome surface — each OTA's actual aperture footprint; if it pokes past the door's edge, that part is vignetted
  • Rust-colored skyline — your local horizon limit, from the Local Horizon card
  • Blue arc — the celestial equator (Dec=0), traced on the dome shell; genuinely tilts with latitude, crossing the horizon due East and due West
  • Bright dot — the elevated celestial pole, at altitude = your latitude on the North (or South) horizon-to-zenith line, where a polar-aligned GEM's RA axis points; HA=0 and GEM meridian flips happen along that same line
