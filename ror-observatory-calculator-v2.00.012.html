Building Shape

Most buildings are a simple rectangle — the width/length above cover that. Non-rectangular buildings (angled corners, an extension, an irregular footprint) can be described directly as up to 6 walls, each its own distance and angle from center.

Angle is measured from North (0°), clockwise — same convention as azimuth. Distance is the perpendicular distance from the building's center out to that wall.

Sky Coverage

Sky Coverage

Sweeps a grid across the whole sky and checks whether each point clears the walls, the parked roof, and your local horizon — not a dome shutter, since a roll-off roof either fully opens or fully closes.

Sky Coverage
Reading this

Center = zenith, edge = horizon, up = North, right = East. The outer boundary traces your building's actual wall shape — a 4-wall rectangle shows as a rectangle, a 6-wall polygon shows its true footprint. Updates live as you change your setup.

● Green — clear of walls, roof, and horizon
● Red — blocked by a wall or the parked roof
● Brown — below your local (terrain) horizon

Local Horizon

Local Horizon

Terrain beyond the building — trees, other structures. Layered on top of the wall/roof obstruction; whichever blocks more at a given azimuth wins.
